Biography


Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied aerospace engineering. Kalam spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

Awards & Honours

Padma Bhushan

1981

This is the third-highest civilian award in the Republic of India, awarded by the Government of India.

Padma Vibhushan

1990

This is the second-highest civilian award in the Republic of India, also awarded by the Government of India.

Bharat Ratna

1997

This is the highest civilian award in the Republic of India, given in recognition of exceptional service towards the advancement of art.

Indira Gandhi Award

1997

Given for promoting national integration and understanding among different sections of the people of India.

Veer Savarkar Award

1998

Awarded by the Government of India for his outstanding contribution to the promotion of the national ideology and unity.

Ramanujan Award

2000

Conferred by the Alwars Research Centre, Chennai, for his outstanding contribution to the growth of the Indian economy.

Veera Pandiya Kattabomman Award

1998

Given by the Government of Tamil Nadu for excellence in administration

King Charles II Medal

2007

Awarded by the UK-based Royal Society for promoting science and technology in India.
